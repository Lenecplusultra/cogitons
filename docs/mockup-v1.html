What documents do I need for a German student visa from Cameroon?
NK
Nadia K. Posted 2 hours ago
I've been accepted to a university in Berlin starting this October and I'm now beginning the visa application process. I've heard the German student visa is very document-heavy and some people get rejected for small mistakes. I want to make sure I don't miss anything important. Has anyone done this recently from Cameroon? What was the process like and what exactly do you need to bring?
8 responses
DM
Daniel M.✓ Most useful1h ago
Done this in 2024 from Yaoundé. You'll need: (1) university admission letter, (2) proof of financial means — either a blocked account of €11,208 or a scholarship letter, (3) health insurance valid for Germany, (4) passport valid at least 6 months beyond your intended stay, (5) certified academic transcripts, (6) motivation letter in German or English. Book your embassy appointment as early as possible — Yaoundé is typically booked out 6–8 weeks in advance. Start the blocked account process immediately if you're going that route.
AJ
Amara J.45min ago
Adding to Daniel's answer — make sure all your documents are translated to German or English by a certified translator if they're originally in French. The embassy is strict about this. Also bring extra copies of everything on the day of your appointment, not just originals. They sometimes keep copies.

Brigitte F.20min ago
One thing I wish someone had told me: the blocked account (Sperrkonto) with Deutsche Bank takes 2–3 weeks to set up and requires a fee. Don't leave this to the last minute. Fintiba and Expatrio are alternatives that are faster and popular with students.
